During the winter of 2014 I built a website for my fraternity’s yearly weeklong philanthropy: Derby Days. The goals of the website were to demonstrate the purpose of the philanthropy, promote the philanthropy’s schedule and sponsors, and accept credit card donations. The credit card donation functionality (which I implemented using the Stripe API) was the most challenging and rewarding part of this project. It exposed me to PHP for the first time and taught me how to work with third-party API documentation.

In seven days from February 8th to 14th, the website I built brought in over $4,000 of donations which went directly to Lurie Children’s Hospital of Chicago. I’m proud of the work I did creating the website from start to finish and of the positive effect it had on the philanthropy’s fundraising and ultimately the patients at the Children’s Hospital.

While the philanthropy was live, this website was available at derbydaysNU.com. Since I am not in control of the derbydaysNU domain I have linked to heroku above to ensure continued accessibility.
