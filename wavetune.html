About

In the fall of 2013 I worked on a team of three students that designed the WaveTune as part of an engineering design class. Our client, a physical therapist from the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago, asked us to design a musical apparatus to aid in physical therapy sessions for a five-year-old boy with a traumatic brain injury. She asked our team to design a product that would capture the boy’s attention with musical sounds while motivating him to develop the underused muscles in his right arm and neck. Our design, the WaveTune, combined a Theremin and a visualizer in an adjustable stand to accomplish these goals.

During this project I communicated directly with the client to ensure our design would meet her objectives. I designed mockups for design concepts, led team meetings, and wrote reports on our design’s progress. When our team refined our final design, I created orthographic and isometric projections of our design using AutoCAD and constructed the final prototype.

This project culminated in the creation of a 30-page report and a presentation of our design and final prototype to the client.
