During the summer of 2014 I created this website to serve as a personal portfolio, showcasing my academic and creative work while also honing my web development skills. Besides the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript/jQuery involved, I learned version control with Git and familiarized myself with Twitter Bootstrap. You can view the repository for this project, along with all my glorious commits, by clicking on the GitHub button above.

Responsiveness was a must, and I found Twitter Bootstrap to be an excellent tool. The 12-column grid system was effective and allowed me to control exactly how content was displayed on various devices. You’ve likely just looked at the desktop version so I’ve included some screenshots of the mobile formatting in the slideshow on this page. Resize your browser to see for yourself!

I wanted to give visitors to my website a clean, direct experience. I believe that is reflected in the feel and overall minimalist approach of the design. Small touches like the welcome page parallax effect and the auto-hiding nav bar give the site personality without being flashy or obtrusive.

This website will definitely be an ongoing project for me. I’m proud of what I’ve created so far and I can’t wait to see what the future holds!
